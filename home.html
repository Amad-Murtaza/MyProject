Chowmos Festival

The Chawmos Festival is a vibrant winter solstice celebration of the Kalash people in Chitral, Pakistan. Marking the end of the harvest, it features rituals, feasts, dancing, and prayers for prosperity, showcasing the unique culture and spirituality of the Kalash.

Chilamjusht

The Chilamjusht Festival, also known as Joshi, is a lively spring festival celebrated by the Kalash people in Chitral, Pakistan. Held in May, it marks the arrival of spring with colorful attire, traditional dances, music, and rituals to honor deities and pray for a bountiful harvest. This festival is a vibrant expression of Kalash culture and joy.

Uchal Festival

The Uchal Festival is a summer celebration of the Kalash people in Chitral, Pakistan, held in August to mark the seasonal harvest. Featuring traditional songs, dances, and rituals, it honors the Kalash deities and gives thanks for nature‚Äôs bounty. The festival is a vibrant display of the Kalash's unique culture and traditions.

Jashn-E-Qaqlasht

The Qaqlasht Festival is a vibrant spring celebration held on the Qaqlasht plateau near Chitral, Pakistan. It showcases traditional sports like polo, tug-of-war, and archery, alongside cultural performances, music, and dances. Celebrated in April, it promotes the region's heritage and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains, making it a unique blend of culture and nature.

Navroz

Celebrated on March 21, Navroz marks the Persian New Year and the arrival of spring. The festival is observed with family gatherings, traditional foods, and prayers for renewal and prosperity.

Broghil Festival

The Broghil Festival is a unique cultural event held in the Broghil Valley, located in the remote northern region of Chitral, Pakistan, near the Afghanistan border. This festival, typically held in August, celebrates the traditional lifestyle, sports, and cultural heritage of the people living in the valley, including the Khowar and Wakhi communities.
